`CODEOWNERS` is the machine-readable counterpart that drives review routing.
This file is the human-readable record of who is currently active and how to
join.

## How to become a maintainer

A maintainer is anyone who has consistently contributed quality PRs over
several months and is invited by existing maintainers to take on review
responsibility. There is no formal application; the path is:

1. Open PRs that match the project's standards
   (see [`CONTRIBUTING.md`](./CONTRIBUTING.md)).
2. Help triage issues and review others' PRs informally — comments on any PR
   are welcome.
3. After ~3–5 substantive merged PRs, an existing maintainer will reach out
   about taking on review responsibility.

## Stepping down

A maintainer can step down at any time by opening a PR that removes their
entry from this file and from `CODEOWNERS`. No ceremony required.

## Inactivity

A maintainer with no review activity for six months may be moved to an
"emeritus" status by a simple-majority decision of remaining active
maintainers. Emeritus maintainers can return to active status any time
they re-engage; no application process.
