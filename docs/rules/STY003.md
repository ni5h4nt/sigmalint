# STY003 — Four-space indentation (list-item continuation at 4n+2 also accepted)

## What it checks

Every non-empty line's leading indent must be either:

- A multiple of 4 (`0, 4, 8, 12, ...`) — standard YAML mapping nesting at
  4-space steps, OR
- A multiple of 4 plus 2 (`2, 6, 10, 14, ...`) — list-item continuation, where
  subsequent keys of a list-item mapping align with the first key (which sits
  after the `- ` marker).

Tabs are not counted as indentation; if a file uses tabs, STY003 sees zero
leading spaces and does not fire (a separate "no tabs" rule may be added in
v0.2).

## Why

Matches the SigmaHQ canonical style and avoids visual ambiguity around nested
selectors. The 4n+2 allowance is **not** a deviation from the style — it is
the natural consequence of YAML's list-item marker (`- ` takes 2 columns), so
multi-key list-item mappings inherit a +2 offset.

## Bad example

```yaml
detection:
   selection:           # 3-space indent — neither 4n nor 4n+2
       Image: x
   condition: selection
```

## Good examples

Standard 4-space mapping:

```yaml
detection:
    selection:
        Image: x
    condition: selection
```

List-item continuation at 4n+2 (this is the same SigmaHQ-canonical style):

```yaml
related:
    - id: c4042d54-110d-45dd-a0e1-05c47822c937
      type: similar               # 6-space indent — VALID
references:
    - https://example.com/a       # 4-space indent of `- ` marker
    - https://example.com/b
```

## How to fix

Reformat with a YAML-aware tool (`yamlfmt`, `prettier --parser yaml`). Fixing
by hand is also fine — every indent should advance by exactly 4 spaces from
its parent, and list-item-mapping keys should align with their first key.

## Empirical note

An initial pass of STY003 on the SigmaHQ public corpus (commit
`994da16`, 3,132 rules) showed 1053 firings, of which 1048 were
false positives from the old `indent % 4 != 0` predicate
flagging valid list-item continuations. The remaining 5 were real
off-step indents (3, 5, 9, 15 spaces). The 4n+2 allowance was added
in response.
